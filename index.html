Jean Barré

Hi, Welcome to my webpage!

I am Jean Barré, a first-year Ph.D. candidate under the supervision of Thierry Poibeau, and Thomas Conrad at École Normale Supérieure / Paris Sciences Lettres (France).

My thesis is part of the new research perspectives opened by machine learning and NLP methods applied to the analysis of literary texts. The aim is to explore the literary past in distant reading, with computationnal methods on massive and digitized corpora.
I am working on the notion of Canonicity and its canonization process. The question I am trying to address is wether we can link canonicity with textual dynamics and wether they can be seen as causal or as the result of biased latent selection mechanisms.
I am also working on the notion of transtextuality, i.e. all the relationships (imitation, influence, subversion) of one text with all others. I'm particularly interested in novelistic sub-genres as crystallizations of this transtextuality.
The project's ambition is to establish synchronic models that, through comparison the history of the formal evolution of literature.

I am an engineer by training. I gratuated from the master degree "Digital Humanities" at École nationale des Chartes in 2022.
